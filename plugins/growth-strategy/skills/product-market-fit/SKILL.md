# Product-Market Fit

## Overview
The degree to which a product satisfies strong market demand, indicating the foundation for sustainable growth.

## PMF Definition

### Marc Andreessen
"Being in a good market with a product that can satisfy that market"

### Sean Ellis Test
**"How would you feel if you could no longer use this product?"**
- 40%+ answering "very disappointed" = PMF achieved

### Rahul Vohra (Superhuman)
Measure and improve PMF score systematically through survey methodology

## PMF Indicators

### Leading Indicators
- High retention rates
- Organic growth
- Word-of-mouth referrals
- Users telling you what to build next
- Struggling to keep up with demand

### Lagging Indicators
- Low churn (<5% monthly for B2C, <2% for B2B)
- Strong NPS (>50)
- Healthy LTV:CAC ratio (>3:1)
- Exponential growth curve

## PMF Framework Stages

### Pre-PMF
**Focus**: Finding PMF
- Rapid iteration
- Customer discovery
- Hypothesis testing
- Small cohorts
- Learning velocity

### At PMF
**Focus**: Optimization
- Improving unit economics
- Scaling acquisition
- Retention optimization
- Feature refinement

### Post-PMF
**Focus**: Growth
- Market expansion
- New segments
- Geographic expansion
- Product extensions

## Measurement Methods

### Sean Ellis Survey
1. "How would you feel if you could no longer use [product]?"
   - Very disappointed
   - Somewhat disappointed
   - Not disappointed

2. What type of people benefit most from [product]?
3. What is the main benefit you receive from [product]?
4. How can we improve [product]?

### Superhuman PMF Engine
1. **Segment users** by "very disappointed"
2. **Analyze high-expectation customers** (your best fit)
3. **Double down** on what they love
4. **Address gaps** in their needs
5. **Ignore detractors** who aren't target segment

## Path to PMF

### 1. Market Understanding
- Identify underserved needs
- Define target segment
- Understand alternatives
- Map competitive landscape

### 2. Value Proposition
- Core value hypothesis
- Must-have vs. nice-to-have
- Unique differentiation
- Emotional and functional benefits

### 3. MVP Validation
- Build minimum viable product
- Get in users' hands quickly
- Measure engagement
- Iterate based on feedback

### 4. Traction Metrics
- Define success metrics
- Set PMF targets
- Track cohort behavior
- Monitor retention curves

### 5. Iteration Loops
- Build-measure-learn cycles
- Customer feedback integration
- Feature prioritization
- Continuous testing

## Maintaining PMF

### Market Evolution
- Monitor market changes
- Competitive responses
- Technology shifts
- Customer needs evolution

### Expansion Strategy
- Adjacent markets
- New customer segments
- Product line extensions
- Geographic expansion

## Anti-Patterns
- Scaling before PMF (premature scaling)
- Ignoring retention for acquisition
- Building for everyone
- Feature bloat without focus
- Assuming PMF is permanent

## When to Use
Startup validation, new product launches, market expansion, strategic pivots
