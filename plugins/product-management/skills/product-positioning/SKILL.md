# Product Positioning

## Overview
Strategic frameworks for defining how your product is perceived in the market and differentiating from competitors.

## Positioning Statement Framework
**For** [target customer]
**Who** [customer need]
**The** [product name] is a [product category]
**That** [key benefit]
**Unlike** [competition]
**Our product** [key differentiation]

## Value Proposition Canvas
### Customer Profile
- Jobs to be done
- Pains (obstacles, risks, bad outcomes)
- Gains (desired outcomes, benefits)

### Value Map
- Products & Services
- Pain relievers
- Gain creators

## Differentiation Strategies
1. **Feature differentiation** - Unique capabilities
2. **Quality differentiation** - Superior performance
3. **Service differentiation** - Better customer experience
4. **Price differentiation** - Cost leadership or premium
5. **Brand differentiation** - Emotional connection

## April Dunford's Positioning Framework
1. Competitive alternatives
2. Unique attributes
3. Value (and proof)
4. Target market characteristics
5. Market category
6. Relevant trends

## When to Use
New product launches, rebranding, competitive repositioning, messaging development
