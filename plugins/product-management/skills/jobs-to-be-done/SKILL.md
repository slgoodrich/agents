# Jobs-to-be-Done (JTBD)

## Overview
Framework for understanding why customers "hire" products to accomplish specific jobs in their lives.

## Core Concepts

### Job Statement Format
**When** [situation]
**I want to** [motivation]
**So I can** [expected outcome]

### Job Types
1. **Functional Jobs** - Practical tasks to accomplish
2. **Emotional Jobs** - How customers want to feel
3. **Social Jobs** - How customers want to be perceived

## JTBD Interview Framework

### Discovery Questions
- "Tell me about the last time you [switched products]"
- "What were you hoping would happen?"
- "What was the struggle?"
- "What did you try before this?"

### Forces Diagram
**Push** (problems with current solution)
**Pull** (attraction of new solution)
**Anxiety** (concerns about new solution)
**Habit** (comfort with current solution)

## Jobs Ladder
- **Higher level**: Life goals and aspirations
- **Middle level**: Functional jobs
- **Lower level**: Specific tasks and features

## Outcome-Driven Innovation
- Desired outcomes (what customers measure)
- Importance (how important is outcome)
- Satisfaction (how well current solutions deliver)
- Opportunity = Importance + (Importance - Satisfaction)

## When to Use
Product discovery, market segmentation, innovation opportunities, feature prioritization
