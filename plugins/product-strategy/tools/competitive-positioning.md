# Competitive Positioning Analysis

Comprehensive competitive positioning analysis and strategy development using multiple frameworks.

## Usage

**Context from command**: $ARGUMENTS

**Model Override**: Use `--model sonnet` for higher quality analysis (default: varies by tool).

If $ARGUMENTS is provided, use it as starting context. Otherwise, gather:
- **Your product/company**: What are you positioning?
- **Market/category**: What market are you in?
- **Key competitors**: 3-5 main competitors (or describe competitive landscape)
- **Target customer**: Primary customer segment
- **Current position** (optional): Where you are now

## Output

Multi-dimensional competitive positioning analysis:

### 1. Competitive Landscape Overview

**Market Map**:
- All significant competitors
- Market segments they serve
- Their positioning and differentiation
- Market share estimates
- Growth trajectories

**Competitor Profiles**:
For each major competitor:
- Core value proposition
- Target customers
- Key strengths
- Key weaknesses
- Pricing and business model
- Go-to-market approach
- Recent moves and trajectory

### 2. Positioning Framework Analysis

**April Dunford's Positioning Framework**:

1. **Competitive Alternatives**
   - What would customers use if you didn't exist?
   - Status quo, DIY solutions, direct competitors

2. **Unique Attributes**
   - Features/capabilities only you have
   - What makes you different (not better, different)

3. **Value (and Proof)**
   - What value do unique attributes enable?
   - Why do attributes matter to customers?
   - Evidence and proof points

4. **Target Market Characteristics**
   - Who cares most about your unique value?
   - Best-fit customer attributes
   - Segment prioritization

5. **Market Category**
   - What market are you in?
   - Category definition
   - Category creation vs. category entry

6. **Relevant Trends**
   - Market shifts that make you more relevant
   - Trends that validate your approach

**Positioning Statement**:
Clear, concise positioning statement based on framework

### 3. Perceptual Positioning Map

**2x2 Positioning Matrix**:
- X-axis: [Key dimension 1, e.g., Ease of Use]
- Y-axis: [Key dimension 2, e.g., Power/Features]
- Plot your product and competitors
- Identify white space and crowded areas

**Alternative Maps** (if relevant):
- Price vs. Features
- Horizontal (broad) vs. Vertical (specialized)
- Traditional vs. Modern
- Self-serve vs. High-touch

### 4. Feature Comparison Matrix

**Competitive Feature Grid**:
| Feature/Capability | Your Product | Competitor A | Competitor B | Competitor C |
| Unique differentiators
| Core capabilities
| Table stakes features
| Missing capabilities

**Analysis**:
- Where you lead
- Where you're at parity
- Where you lag
- Which gaps matter most

### 5. Differentiation Strategy

**Your Unique Value Propositions**:
- Primary differentiation
- Secondary differentiation
- Tertiary differentiation

**Defensibility**:
- How hard to copy?
- Network effects or moats
- Sustainable advantages

**Trade-offs You Make**:
- What you deliberately DON'T do
- Who you deliberately DON'T serve
- Why trade-offs create advantage

### 6. Competitive Strategy Recommendations

**Positioning Strategy**:
- Head-to-head vs. differentiation
- Category leadership vs. niche domination
- Positioning in customer's mind

**Messaging Recommendations**:
- Primary message
- Proof points
- Customer segments and messaging
- Competitive comparisons

**Product Strategy**:
- Features to build (extend lead)
- Features to match (table stakes)
- Features to ignore (strategic trade-offs)

**Go-to-Market Strategy**:
- Where competitors are weak
- Underserved segments
- Channel opportunities

### 7. Competitive Threats & Opportunities

**Threats**:
- Competitor moves to watch
- Market shifts that help competitors
- Your vulnerabilities

**Opportunities**:
- Competitor weaknesses to exploit
- Market gaps to fill
- Trends that favor you

**Competitive Moat**:
- How to build sustainable advantage
- Defensive strategies
- Offense strategies

**Leader**:
- Emphasize market leadership
- Set standards
- Full-featured

**Challenger**:
- Better alternative to leader
- Specific advantages
- Aggressive positioning

**Specialist**:
- Deep in niche
- Best for specific use case
- Expertise and focus

**Disruptor**:
- New approach
- Challenge status quo
- Modern vs. legacy

## Tips for Effective Positioning

**Do**:
- Be specific and focused
- Own a clear position
- Be authentic and credible
- Choose battles wisely
- Validate with customers

**Don't**:
- Try to be everything to everyone
- Claim superiority on all dimensions
- Copy competitor positioning
- Ignore your actual strengths
- Position based on features alone

## Maintaining Competitive Position

- **Monitor competitors** - Weekly/monthly reviews
- **Update positioning** - Quarterly reviews
- **Customer validation** - Do they perceive you this way?
- **Win/loss analysis** - Why you win or lose
- **Market changes** - Trends that affect position

Positioning is not static - evolve as market and product evolve.
