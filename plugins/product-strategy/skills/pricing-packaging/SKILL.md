# Pricing & Packaging

## Overview
Strategic frameworks for determining pricing models, creating product tiers, and optimizing revenue.

## Pricing Strategies

### Value-Based Pricing
Price based on value delivered to customer
- **Willingness to pay** - What customers value
- **Value metrics** - Align price with usage
- **Segmentation** - Different values for different segments

### Cost-Plus Pricing
Price based on costs + margin
- Simple to calculate
- Ignores customer value
- Common in commodities

### Competition-Based Pricing
Price relative to competitors
- Market positioning
- Feature parity consideration
- Race to bottom risk

### Penetration Pricing
Low initial price to gain market share
- Quick adoption
- Network effects acceleration
- Future price increases

### Premium Pricing
High price for premium positioning
- Quality signal
- Brand building
- Limited addressable market

## Pricing Models

### SaaS Pricing Models

**Per-User Pricing**
- $X per user per month
- Easy to understand
- Encourages limited sharing

**Usage-Based Pricing**
- Pay for what you use
- Aligns with customer value
- Variable revenue

**Flat-Rate Pricing**
- Unlimited usage for flat fee
- Simple, predictable
- May leave money on table

**Tiered Pricing**
- Good/Better/Best
- Captures different segments
- Upgrade path

**Freemium**
- Free basic tier
- Paid advanced features
- High conversion challenge

**Free Trial**
- Limited time access
- Test before buying
- Conversion pressure

## Packaging Strategy

### Good/Better/Best
**Three-Tier Model**
- **Good** - Entry level, limited features
- **Better** - Most popular, best value
- **Best** - Premium, all features

### Packaging Principles
1. **Clear differentiation** - Obvious value between tiers
2. **Upgrade path** - Natural progression
3. **Anchor pricing** - Expensive tier makes middle attractive
4. **Feature fencing** - Limit by capabilities, not artificial constraints
5. **Land and expand** - Start small, grow over time

### Value Metrics
What you charge for:
- **Users** - Per seat
- **Usage** - API calls, storage, transactions
- **Outcomes** - Leads generated, revenue enabled
- **Features** - Advanced capabilities
- **Support** - SLA, dedicated success

## Pricing Psychology

### Pricing Tactics
- **Charm pricing** - $99 vs $100
- **Anchoring** - Show higher price first
- **Decoy pricing** - Third option to guide choice
- **Bundle pricing** - Package discount
- **Price framing** - $10/day vs $300/month

### Positioning Tiers
- Make middle tier most attractive (2x value)
- Premium tier 3-5x middle tier
- Limit free tier to encourage upgrade
- Annual discount (15-20%)

## Pricing Experiments

### Testing Approaches
- **A/B test prices** - New customers only
- **Segment testing** - Different markets
- **Grandfather existing** - Protect current customers
- **Survey willingness to pay** - Before launching

### Pricing Changes
- Announce in advance
- Grandfather existing customers (option)
- Explain value justification
- Provide migration path
- Monitor churn carefully

## Monetization Metrics

### Key Metrics
- **ARPU** - Average revenue per user
- **ARPA** - Average revenue per account
- **Conversion rate** - Free to paid
- **Expansion revenue** - Upsells, cross-sells
- **LTV:CAC ratio** - Unit economics
- **Payback period** - Months to recover CAC

### Cohort Analysis
- Revenue retention by cohort
- Expansion rate
- Contraction rate
- Logo retention vs. revenue retention

## B2B vs. B2C Considerations

### B2B
- Higher prices
- Annual contracts
- Custom enterprise plans
- Negotiated pricing
- Procurement process

### B2C
- Self-serve
- Monthly billing
- Credit card only
- Lower prices
- High volume

## Pricing Evolution

### Expansion Strategies
- Add new tiers
- Introduce usage-based elements
- Create enterprise tier
- Add professional services
- Platform/API monetization

### When to Raise Prices
- Strong PMF achieved
- Delivering more value
- Market position strengthened
- For new customers (grandfather existing)

## When to Use
Go-to-market planning, revenue optimization, product positioning, business model design
