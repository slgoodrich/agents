# Alignment Session Facilitator

Facilitate cross-functional alignment meetings that drive clear decisions, ensure stakeholder buy-in, and prevent endless debate cycles.

## Usage

```
/pm:align [topic] [stakeholders]
```

## What This Command Does

1. **Structures productive alignment meetings** with clear agendas and outcomes (saves 1 hour prep time)
2. **Provides decision frameworks** (DACI, RACI, consensus) to clarify roles and authority
3. **Ensures all stakeholders are heard** through structured discussion and documented input
4. **Creates actionable follow-up plans** with owners and deadlines to drive execution
5. **Prevents "alignment theater"** by forcing concrete decisions and "disagree and commit" culture
6. **Builds trust** through transparent decision-making and inclusive process

## Instructions

**Context from command**: $ARGUMENTS

### 1. Gather Required Information

**IMPORTANT**: If $ARGUMENTS is empty or insufficient, gather this information from the user:

**Decision/Alignment Context**:
- What topic needs alignment? (pricing, roadmap, strategy, org change, feature prioritization)
- What type of alignment? (decision needed, strategic alignment, buy-in required, conflict resolution)
- Why now? (what's the urgency or trigger for this session)
- What happens if we don't align? (cost of delay, impact of continued misalignment)

**Current State**:
- What's the current disagreement or misalignment? (who disagrees on what)
- What's been tried already? (emails, 1:1s, smaller meetings)
- What positions do stakeholders hold? (understand each perspective)
- Is there a deadline? (external driver like customer commitment, board meeting)

**Stakeholders**:
- Who MUST be in the room? (decision-maker, key contributors)
- Who should provide input? (subject matter experts, affected teams)
- Who should be informed? (don't need to attend but care about outcome)
- Who's the Approver? (who has final decision authority - critical to identify)

**Desired Outcome**:
- What specific decision needs to be made? (be very specific)
- What does success look like? (everyone aligned, clear next steps, no re-litigation)
- What alternatives are on the table? (2-5 options to consider)
- What decision criteria matter? (speed, cost, quality, risk, user impact)

**Constraints**:
- How much time do we have? (30 min, 60 min, 90 min - affects depth)
- When does decision need to be made? (today, this week, this month)
- What pre-work can stakeholders do? (read docs, analyze data, prepare positions)
- Any non-negotiables? (company policy, legal requirements, strategic commitments)

### 2. Structure the Session for Success

**Pre-Meeting (Critical - 80% of success)**:

1. **Share Context Document 24-48 hours before**:
   - What we're deciding and why
   - Options being considered
   - Decision criteria
   - Data/analysis supporting each option
   - Questions for stakeholders to consider

2. **Gather Async Input**:
   - Ask stakeholders to comment on doc
   - Surface concerns before meeting
   - Identify areas of agreement (don't waste meeting time)
   - Spot potential blockers early

3. **Identify Decision Framework**:
   - Choose DACI, RACI, or consensus
   - Clearly define roles
   - Get approver to commit to making decision

4. **Prepare Facilitator**:
   - Neutral party (not decision-maker)
   - Prepared to enforce timeboxes
   - Has parking lot for tangents
   - Ready to call out alignment theater

**During Meeting**:

**60-Min Meeting Structure** (adjust for length):
- 0-5 min: Frame decision, process, roles
- 5-20 min: Present options (3-5 min each)
- 20-40 min: Discussion, questions, trade-offs
- 40-50 min: Decision & rationale
- 50-60 min: Next steps, owners, communication plan

**90-Min Meeting Structure** (for complex decisions):
- 0-10 min: Frame decision, level-set context
- 10-35 min: Deep dive on options (8-10 min each with Q&A)
- 35-70 min: Discussion, debate, trade-off analysis
- 70-80 min: Decision & rationale
- 80-90 min: Next steps, ownership, communication

**After Meeting**:
- Document decision and rationale within 24 hours
- Share with all stakeholders (attendees + informed group)
- Track follow-up actions
- No re-litigation (enforce "disagree and commit")

### 3. Choose the Right Decision Framework

**DACI Framework** (Recommended for most decisions):
- **Driver**: Runs the process, gathers input, synthesizes, recommends (often PM)
- **Approver**: Makes final decision, owns outcome (one person only)
- **Contributors**: Provide input, subject matter expertise (consulted)
- **Informed**: Told of decision after it's made (don't attend meeting)

**Use DACI when**:
- Need clear decision authority
- Strategic or significant investment decision
- Multiple stakeholders but one decision-maker
- Time-sensitive decision

**RACI Framework** (For work distribution):
- **Responsible**: Does the work
- **Accountable**: Owns the outcome (one person)
- **Consulted**: Provides input (two-way communication)
- **Informed**: Kept in loop (one-way communication)

**Use RACI when**:
- Assigning work across teams
- Cross-functional project launch
- Clarifying ownership confusion
- Process or workflow alignment

**Consensus Building** (For culture/team decisions):
- Everyone has equal voice
- Discussion until everyone can support decision
- Not unanimity - but everyone can commit
- Slower but higher buy-in

**Use Consensus when**:
- Team culture decisions
- Values or principles
- Changes affecting everyone equally
- Buy-in more critical than speed

### 4. Facilitate Effective Discussion

**Techniques to Keep Discussion Productive**:

**Separate Inquiry from Advocacy**:
- First, ask questions to understand (inquiry)
- Then, present your position (advocacy)
- Don't start by defending your view

**Use "Yes, And" vs "Yes, But"**:
- Build on others' ideas
- Find synthesis, not winners/losers

**Timebox Ruthlessly**:
- "We have 10 minutes for this topic"
- Use visible timer
- Parking lot for tangents

**Call Out Alignment Theater**:
- "We're discussing but not deciding"
- "What specific decision are we making?"
- "This feels like we're avoiding the hard choice"

**Surface Underlying Concerns**:
- "What's the real concern here?"
- "What would need to be true for you to support Option A?"
- Disagreement often about different assumptions

**Use Decision Criteria to Resolve**:
- "Which option scores highest on our criteria?"
- "Let's evaluate against what we said matters"
- Removes emotion, focuses on logic

### 5. Handle Common Dysfunctions

**Issue: No One Wants to Decide**
- **Symptom**: Endless discussion, no closure
- **Fix**: Identify Approver beforehand, give them deadline to decide
- **Escalate**: If Approver won't decide, escalate to their manager

**Issue: Steamrolling (Loudest Voice Wins)**
- **Symptom**: Senior person dominates, others silent
- **Fix**: Round-robin input, anonymous pre-work, explicit "I want to hear from..."
- **Facilitator Role**: Protect quieter voices

**Issue: Relitigating Past Decisions**
- **Symptom**: "We already decided this" / reopening closed topics
- **Fix**: Document original decision + rationale, enforce "disagree and commit"
- **When to Reopen**: Only if significant new information emerged

**Issue: Analysis Paralysis**
- **Symptom**: "We need more data" indefinitely
- **Fix**: Set decision deadline, identify minimum data needed, make decision with uncertainty
- **Reality**: Most decisions are reversible, decide and learn

**Issue: False Consensus**
- **Symptom**: Everyone nods but no real alignment
- **Fix**: Explicit check: "Can everyone commit to this?" vs "Does everyone agree?"
- **Disagree and Commit**: I may not agree, but I'll support the decision

**Issue: Decision Avoidance**
- **Symptom**: Meeting ends with "let's think about it"
- **Fix**: Don't end meeting without decision or explicit next step with owner + deadline
- **Escalate**: If repeated, escalate to leadership

## Discussion Ground Rules

**Establish at Start**:

1. **Disagree and Commit**: Voice concerns fully, then support decision (not consensus)
2. **Data Over Opinions**: Use facts when available, label opinions as opinions
3. **One Conversation**: Decision made today is final, no relitigating unless new data
4. **Respect Time**: Honor timeboxes, use parking lot for tangents
5. **Assume Positive Intent**: Everyone wants what's best for company
6. **Equal Voice**: Everyone gets heard, seniority doesn't = louder voice
7. **Decision by [Time]**: Approver commits to deciding before meeting ends

**Parking Lot** (for tangents):
- [Topic 1 to revisit later]
- [Topic 2 to revisit later]

## Best Practices

### DO ✅

- **Share pre-read 24-48hrs ahead** - No surprises in meeting, async input gathered
- **Use decision framework** - DACI, RACI, or consensus explicitly stated upfront
- **Set clear success criteria** - What does alignment look like? Everyone commits.
- **Enforce timeboxes strictly** - Respect everyone's time, keep discussion focused
- **Document decision + rationale** - Write down what was decided and why within 24 hours
- **Assign owners to all actions** - Every next step needs a name and specific deadline
- **Get "disagree and commit"** - Not consensus, but commitment to support decision
- **Communicate broadly** - Tell everyone affected by decision using clear plan
- **Use data to decide** - Facts beat opinions, criteria beat politics
- **Call out alignment theater** - If discussing without deciding, call it out

### DON'T ❌

- **Don't make it a presentation** - Should be discussion and decision, not lecture
- **Don't avoid the decision** - Must walk out with clear decision or explicit next step with owner
- **Don't let loudest voice win** - Use framework to ensure all perspectives heard equally
- **Don't relitigate later** - Once decided, enforce "disagree and commit" rule
- **Don't skip follow-up** - Track actions to completion, decisions without execution are useless
- **Don't forget the "why"** - Document rationale so future you understands context
- **Don't have too many people** - >8 attendees = too many, move some to "informed"
- **Don't start without Approver** - If decision-maker can't attend, reschedule
- **Don't make decisions by committee** - One approver, not voting or consensus (unless explicit)
- **Don't go over time** - If out of time, schedule follow-up vs dragging on
